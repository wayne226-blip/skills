# Follow-Up Sequence Writer

Write follow-up emails to chase quotes for Dave's Plumbing & Heating. This is Dave's biggest pain point -- he loses about half his jobs because he forgets to follow up after sending a quote.

## Rules
- Read BRAND.md for tone before writing
- Always sign off with: Cheers, Dave
- British English only
- Friendly, not pushy -- Dave's a mate, not a salesman
- First person ("I" not "we") -- Dave is a sole trader
- Keep emails short -- 3-5 sentences max. Customers don't want an essay
- Never use pressure tactics or fake urgency
- Never use: "honestly", "cheap", "ASAP", corporate jargon
- Always use: natural, conversational language like "give me a shout", "happy to help", "no problem"
- Reference the specific job/quote where possible (the user will provide details)

## Sequence Pattern

### Email 1 -- Friendly check-in (send 2-3 days after quote)
- Subject line: casual, reference the job
- Quick check they got the quote
- Ask if they have any questions
- No pressure -- just touching base
- Keep it to 3-4 sentences

### Email 2 -- Helpful nudge (send 7-10 days after quote)
- Reference the original quote
- Add something useful (e.g., "happy to pop round again if you want to talk it through")
- Mention availability ("I've got some space in my diary next week")
- Still no pressure

### Email 3 -- Door open (send 14 days after quote)
- Final follow-up -- acknowledge they might have gone elsewhere or decided not to go ahead
- Keep the door open ("no worries either way, just give me a shout if you change your mind")
- Short and warm

## Output Format

For each email in the sequence:

```
**Email [number] -- Send [timing]**

**Subject:** [subject line]

[Body text]

Cheers, Dave
```

## Example Tone

Good: "Hi Sarah, just checking you got the quote I sent over for the bathroom. Any questions, give me a shout -- happy to pop round and talk it through."

Bad: "Dear Mrs Johnson, I am writing to follow up on quotation ref #2847 submitted on 15th March. Please do not hesitate to contact us at your earliest convenience."
