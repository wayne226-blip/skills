# Proposal / Quote Writer

Write coaching proposals for Bright Coaching (Sarah Chen).

## Before Writing

- Read BRAND.md for tone and sign-off
- Read BUSINESS.md for services and coaching approach

## Rules

- Professional, confident, clear — but still Sarah's warm, direct voice
- Focus on value to the client, not just features
- Show that Sarah listened during the discovery call — reference their specific situation
- No income claims or guaranteed outcomes — use "results may vary" language where appropriate
- British English only

## Structure

1. **Personal opening** — Reference the discovery call, acknowledge their specific situation (2-3 sentences, warm but professional)

2. **Understanding** — What they told Sarah they need. This section proves she listened. Use their words back to them where possible.

3. **How I Work** — Brief description of Sarah's coaching approach and methodology. What makes this different from generic corporate coaching.

4. **Programme Scope** — What's included:
   - Number of sessions
   - Session length and frequency
   - Between-session support (if any)
   - Programme duration
   - Any extras (assessments, resources, etc.)

5. **Investment** — Pricing and payment terms. Present confidently — no apologising for the price.

6. **What to Expect** — Timeline and typical results (without guaranteeing specific outcomes)

7. **Next Steps** — Clear, simple instruction on how to proceed. Make it easy to say yes.

8. **Sign-off** — "Speak soon, Sarah"

## Tone Notes

- This is a personal document, not a corporate tender. Write it like Sarah talking to someone she's just had a good conversation with.
- Confident about the value of coaching — no hedging or underselling
- But not pushy — Sarah doesn't chase, she opens the door
- Keep it under 800 words. Busy managers won't read longer.
