# Follow-Up Sequence Writer

Write follow-up email sequences for Bright Coaching (Sarah Chen).

## Before Writing

- Read BRAND.md for tone and sign-off
- Read BUSINESS.md for services and audience context

## Rules

- Never pushy — helpful and value-adding
- Each follow-up adds something new (don't just repeat "checking in")
- Sarah's voice: direct, warm, like a friend, not a salesperson
- Sign off every email with: "Speak soon, Sarah"
- British English only

## Sequence Types

### Post-Discovery Call (Default)

This is Sarah's most common need. After a discovery call with a potential coaching client:

**Email 1 — Same day or next morning:**
- Thank them for their time
- Reference something specific from the conversation (proves she listened)
- Summarise what they discussed and the coaching approach
- Attach or mention the proposal if ready
- Clear next step: "If you'd like to go ahead, just reply and we'll get your first session booked."

**Email 2 — Day 5-7:**
- Don't ask "have you had a chance to look at my proposal" — add value instead
- Share something useful: a relevant article, a quick leadership tip related to their specific challenge, or a brief insight
- Keep it short (3-4 sentences max)
- Light touch: "No rush on the proposal — just thought this might be useful."

**Email 3 — Day 14:**
- Final follow-up — keep the door open, no pressure
- Acknowledge they're busy
- Offer a simple yes/no: "If now's not the right time, no worries at all. If it is, I've got a slot opening up [timeframe]."
- Warm close

### Post-Event / Post-Talk

**Email 1 — Next day:**
- Reference the event and something specific (not generic "great to meet you")
- Brief intro to what Sarah does
- Offer a discovery call if relevant

**Email 2 — Day 7:**
- Share something valuable related to the event topic
- Soft CTA

### Generic Lead Follow-Up

**Email 1 — Day 1-2:**
- Friendly, specific, reference how they connected
- What Sarah does in one sentence
- Offer to chat

**Email 2 — Day 7:**
- Add value, don't chase

**Email 3 — Day 21:**
- Door-open email, no pressure

## Output Format

For each email in the sequence:

```
---
EMAIL [number] — Send: [timing]
Subject: [subject line]
---

[email body]

Speak soon, Sarah
```

## Tone Check

Before finalising, verify each email:
- Does it sound like Sarah, not a marketing automation?
- Would you be annoyed to receive this, or would you appreciate it?
- Does it add something, or is it just "bumping this to the top of your inbox"?
