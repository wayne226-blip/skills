# Follow-Up Sequence Writer

Write follow-up email sequences for Bright Coaching.

## Rules
- Read BRAND.md for tone
- Write as Sarah — first person, warm, direct
- Never pushy — helpful and value-adding
- Each follow-up adds something new (don't just repeat "checking in")
- Always sign off with: Speak soon, Sarah
- British English only
- Do not guarantee coaching outcomes

## Sequence Pattern

### Post-Discovery Call (default sequence)
1. **Same day / next morning**: Warm follow-up referencing specific things from the call. Attach proposal if discussed. Keep it personal — mention something they said
2. **Day 5-7**: Add value — share a relevant article, insight, or quick tip related to their challenge. Don't mention the proposal directly
3. **Day 14**: Gentle check-in — acknowledge they're busy, keep the door open, no pressure. Offer to answer any questions
4. **(Optional) Day 30**: Reconnect with a new angle — a relevant post Sarah wrote, a client win story (anonymised), or a new programme launch

### Post-Event / Networking
1. **Next day**: "Great to meet you" + reference to what was discussed + offer to continue the conversation
2. **Day 7**: Share something useful related to what they talked about
3. **Day 14**: Suggest a call if there's potential fit

## Output Format
For each email in the sequence:
1. **Timing** — when to send (e.g., "Day 1 — same day as discovery call")
2. **Subject line**
3. **Body** — complete email, ready to send
4. **Sign-off** — Speak soon, Sarah

## Tone guidance
- These should feel like emails from a real person, not a drip campaign
- Reference specific details from the conversation (use placeholders like [their challenge] or [topic discussed] where Sarah needs to fill in)
- Short and scannable — nobody reads long follow-up emails
