# BRAND DNA DOCUMENT

## BRAND OVERVIEW

- **Name:** Grind
- **URL:** grfrnd.com
- **Tagline:** "Better Coffee. Better For The Planet."
- **Design Agency:** Founded by David Abrahamovitch; branding developed in-house and with London design partners. Known for a distinctive minimalist aesthetic rooted in their Shoreditch cafe origins.
- **Voice Adjectives:** Minimal, confident, sustainable, urban-cool, unpretentious
- **Positioning:** Premium London-born coffee brand that makes specialty-grade coffee accessible through compostable Nespresso-compatible pods, ground coffee, and whole beans — all with a strong sustainability message. They bridge the gap between third-wave coffee quality and everyday convenience.
- **Competitive Differentiation:** Grind was one of the first UK coffee brands to offer fully compostable coffee pods (home-compostable, plastic-free). They combine a cool London hospitality brand (restaurants/cafes in Shoreditch, Holborn, etc.) with a strong DTC subscription model. Their visual identity is far more fashion/lifestyle than typical coffee brands.

## VISUAL SYSTEM

- **Primary Font:** Custom sans-serif (clean, geometric, modern — similar to Futura/Proxima Nova family). Used in uppercase for headers.
- **Secondary Font:** Clean serif for body copy and editorial moments.
- **Primary Color:** Deep black (#000000) — used extensively in typography and packaging
- **Secondary Color:** Off-white / warm cream (#F5F0EB) — backgrounds, negative space
- **Accent Colors:** Muted pastels per product line — dusty pink (#D4A5A5), sage green (#A8B5A2), warm terracotta (#C4836A), soft lavender (#B8A9C9), muted gold (#C4A96A). Each coffee blend gets its own colorway.
- **Background Colors:** Warm cream (#F5F0EB), soft white, occasionally deep black for contrast
- **CTA Color and Style:** Black buttons with white text, occasionally inverted. Clean rounded rectangles.

## PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION

- **Lighting:** Soft, natural, warm — often golden-hour tones. Studio shots are clean and even with soft shadows.
- **Color Grading:** Warm, slightly desaturated, matte finish. Never oversaturated. Earthy, organic tones.
- **Composition:** Minimal, lots of negative space. Product-centered with breathing room. Flatlay and straight-on shots common.
- **Subject Matter:** Coffee moments — morning rituals, kitchen counters, cafe settings. Lifestyle shots feel editorial, not stock.
- **Props and Surfaces:** Marble counters, linen textures, ceramic mugs, natural wood. Clean, considered, never cluttered.
- **Mood:** Calm, elevated everyday, quietly luxurious

## PRODUCT DETAILS

- **Physical Description:** Coffee pods in slim rectangular boxes (10-pack), coffee bags in matte-finish pouches with a flat bottom. Each product variant has a distinct muted pastel color on packaging with the GRIND wordmark in bold uppercase.
- **Label-Logo Placement:** "GRIND" wordmark prominently centered or top-center on all packaging. Bold, uppercase, geometric sans-serif.
- **Distinctive Features:** Compostable pods — this is a major visual and messaging differentiator. The pods themselves are designed to look premium. Packaging uses muted colour-coding per blend (e.g., House Blend, Dark Blend, Decaf each have their own pastel).
- **Packaging System:** Subscription boxes, individual pod boxes (10s, 20s, 100s), ground coffee bags, whole bean bags. Consistent minimal design language across all.

## AD CREATIVE STYLE

- **Typical formats:** Clean product hero shots, lifestyle kitchen/cafe moments, subscription box unboxing, sustainability-led messaging
- **Text overlay style:** Minimal — bold uppercase headlines, lots of white space, rarely more than one line of copy on an image
- **Photo vs illustration:** Photography dominant. No illustration.
- **UGC usage:** Some, but curated and on-brand. Not raw/messy UGC.
- **Offer presentation:** Clean and understated — "First box free" or "Subscribe & Save" style. Never shouty discount stickers.

## COMPETITIVE CONTEXT

- **Competitor 1: Pact Coffee** — More utilitarian design, kraft-paper aesthetic, emphasis on direct-trade sourcing. Less fashion-forward than Grind.
- **Competitor 2: Blue Bottle Coffee** — Similar minimalism but more West Coast/Japanese influenced. Less sustainability messaging.
- **Competitor 3: Nespresso** — The incumbent. Luxury positioning but corporate. Grind positions as the cooler, greener alternative.
