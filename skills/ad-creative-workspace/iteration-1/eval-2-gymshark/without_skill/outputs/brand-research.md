# Gymshark — Brand Research

## Brand Overview
- **Website:** gymshark.com
- **Category:** Fitness apparel and accessories
- **Founded:** 2012 by Ben Francis in Birmingham, UK (started from a garage)
- **Valuation:** Over $1 billion (unicorn status reached ~2020)
- **Tagline:** "Be a Visionary" / "United We Sweat"

## Key Products
- Performance training shorts, joggers, leggings
- Seamless leggings and sports bras (hero products)
- Fitted t-shirts, stringers, tank tops
- Hoodies and pullovers
- Training accessories (bags, bottles, lifting accessories)
- Key lines: Vital Seamless, Adapt, Legacy, Sport, Crest

## Visual Identity
- Bold, high-contrast photography — dramatic gym lighting, sweat-glistening skin
- Dark backgrounds (blacks, charcoals) with vibrant accent colors
- Brand colors: Black primary, with seasonal pops of teal, berry, mauve, rust, neon
- Shark head logo — clean, minimal, recognizable
- Typography: bold sans-serif, uppercase for impact
- Photography style: close-crop muscle detail, dynamic movement, intense expressions
- Social-first content — vertical video, UGC-style mixed with polished studio

## Target Audience
- Gen Z and young millennials, 16-30
- Gym-goers, weightlifters, functional fitness enthusiasts
- Social media native — Instagram, TikTok, YouTube
- Aspirational but accessible price point ($25-70 range)
- Community-driven — Gymshark athletes/ambassadors are key
- Both men and women equally targeted

## Competitive Positioning
- Competes with: Nike, Lululemon, Under Armour, Alphalete, YoungLA
- Differentiator: DTC-first, influencer/community-built, more affordable than Lululemon
- Known for flattering fits, especially women's seamless leggings
- Strong presence at fitness expos (Gymshark pop-ups draw massive crowds)

## Brand Voice
- Motivational, high-energy, direct
- "No excuses" mentality without being toxic
- Inclusive of all body types and fitness levels (evolved over time)
- Meme-savvy, culturally aware, speaks internet fluently
- Community-first — athletes and ambassadors feel like peers, not celebrities

## Distribution
- Primarily direct-to-consumer via gymshark.com
- Limited retail presence — some pop-up stores
- Global shipping, strong UK and US markets

## Ad Style Notes
- Heavy use of athlete/influencer content
- Before/after transformations, workout montages
- Product close-ups showing fabric texture, stretch, fit
- Gym environment: iron, chalk, mirrors, rubber flooring
- High energy music and fast cuts in video content
