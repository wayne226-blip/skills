# Aloha Protein Bars — Brand Research

## Brand Overview
- **Website:** aloha.com
- **Category:** Plant-based protein bars and supplements
- **Tagline:** "Goodness grows here" / plant-based, organic focus
- **Founded:** 2014, rebranded from supplements to protein bars around 2017

## Key Selling Points
- USDA Organic certified
- Plant-based protein (brown rice protein, pumpkin seed protein)
- Low sugar (typically 4-5g per bar)
- 14g protein per bar
- Non-GMO, vegan, gluten-free, soy-free, dairy-free
- No artificial ingredients, no sugar alcohols, no stevia
- Real food ingredients (coconut oil, cocoa butter, real chocolate)

## Core Flavors
- Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough
- Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip
- Chocolate Fudge Brownie
- Coconut Chocolate Almond
- Vanilla Almond Crunch
- Chocolate Mint
- Peanut Butter Cup (seasonal/limited)
- Lemon Cashew

## Visual Identity
- Clean, modern packaging
- Earth tones with bold color accents per flavor
- White/cream base packaging with organic-feeling typography
- Photography style: natural light, lifestyle, wellness-oriented
- Brand colors: greens, earth tones, warm neutrals

## Target Audience
- Health-conscious consumers, 25-45
- Plant-based/vegan/flexitarian eaters
- Active lifestyle — gym, yoga, hiking, outdoor activities
- Clean-label shoppers who read ingredient lists
- Parents looking for healthier snack options
- Premium price point ($2.50-3.50 per bar)

## Competitive Positioning
- Competes with: RXBAR, KIND, GoMacro, No Cow, Garden of Life
- Differentiator: Organic + plant-based + genuinely good taste
- Positioned as the "clean" protein bar that doesn't compromise on flavor

## Distribution
- Direct-to-consumer via aloha.com
- Available at Whole Foods, Target, Costco, Amazon
- Subscription model for DTC

## Brand Voice
- Approachable, warm, not preachy about plant-based
- Emphasizes taste alongside nutrition
- Community and wellness oriented
