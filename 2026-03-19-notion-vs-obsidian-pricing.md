### Price Winner: Obsidian

For individuals, it's not close. Obsidian's core app is completely free with zero limitations. Notion's free tier works for solo use but hits walls on file uploads and version history. For teams, Notion wins on value since collaboration is built in — Obsidian's shared vaults through Sync are more limited.

## Feature Comparison

### Note-Taking & Writing

Both tools use markdown, but differently. Obsidian works with plain `.md` files stored locally on your machine. You own your data, you can open it in any text editor, and it'll outlive any company. Notion stores everything in its own format on its own servers. You can export to markdown, but it's not the native storage.

Notion's editor is more polished out of the box — drag-and-drop blocks, embedded databases, toggles, and callouts all work without setup. Obsidian's editor is leaner but endlessly customisable through community plugins and CSS snippets.

**Winner:** Obsidian for writing purists and data ownership. Notion for visual, block-based editing.

### Organisation & Knowledge Management

Notion gives you databases, kanban boards, calendars, galleries, and relational tables — all native. You can build a CRM, project tracker, and wiki in the same workspace. It's closer to a lightweight Airtable than a note-taking app.

Obsidian's strength is linking. Bidirectional links, graph view, and the Dataview plugin let you build a genuine knowledge graph. It rewards you for connecting ideas over time rather than organising them into neat folders upfront.

**Winner:** Notion for structured project management. Obsidian for networked thinking and personal knowledge bases.

### Collaboration

Notion was built for teams. Real-time editing, comments, @mentions, permissions, and shared workspaces all work seamlessly. It's the clear choice if your team needs a single source of truth.

Obsidian is fundamentally a single-player tool. Shared vaults via Sync exist but are basic — no real-time co-editing, no comments, no granular permissions. If collaboration is a core requirement, Obsidian will frustrate you.

**Winner:** Notion, decisively.

### Privacy & Data Ownership

Obsidian stores everything locally as plain files. With Sync enabled, data is end-to-end encrypted — even Obsidian's team can't read your notes. You can also skip Sync entirely and use iCloud, Dropbox, or Git for free.

Notion stores data on its servers. It's not end-to-end encrypted (Enterprise gets zero data retention with LLM providers for AI features, but that's not the same thing). If your notes contain sensitive information — medical records, legal documents, private journals — this matters.

**Winner:** Obsidian, by a wide margin.

### Extensibility

Obsidian's community plugin ecosystem is massive — over 1,500 plugins covering everything from spaced repetition to Kanban boards to advanced templating. You can turn Obsidian into almost anything, but it takes time and tinkering.

Notion has integrations (Slack, Google Drive, GitHub) and a public API, but the in-app customisation is limited compared to Obsidian. You get what Notion ships.

**Winner:** Obsidian for power users. Notion for those who want things to just work.

## Who Should Choose Notion?

Pick Notion if you:
- Work in a team and need real-time collaboration
- Want databases, project management, and docs in one tool
- Prefer a polished, visual interface that works without configuration
- Value AI features built into your workflow (Business plan)
- Don't want to spend time configuring plugins and themes

[Try Notion Free →](https://www.notion.com)

## Who Should Choose Obsidian?

Pick Obsidian if you:
- Care about data ownership and privacy
- Want your notes in plain markdown files you control forever
- Enjoy customising your tools with plugins and themes
- Build personal knowledge bases with heavy interlinking
- Want a powerful free tool with minimal ongoing costs

[Download Obsidian Free →](https://obsidian.md)

## The Bottom Line

These tools solve different problems. Notion is a team workspace that happens to do notes. Obsidian is a personal knowledge tool that stays out of your way. If you collaborate daily and need structure, go Notion. If you think in links, value privacy, and want to own your data, go Obsidian. Many people use both — Obsidian for personal thinking, Notion for team projects.
